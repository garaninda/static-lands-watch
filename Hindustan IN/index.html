Live TV Moment: Barkha Dutt's Question Leaves Arvind Kejriwal Speechless.

Updated on: March 11, 2026 4:54 PM IST
Edited by Poorva Joshi
Share via
Copy link

The broadcast ended in a public scandal surrounding the Indian banking system. During the latest broadcast of the program We The People on NDTV, the host and guest found themselves face to face. On one side: Barkha Dutt, journalist and host of "We The People". On the other: Arvind Kejriwal, activist and politician. What caused the confrontation? At first, the topic appeared routine: "How to protect your savings in 2026?" But it was this very topic that sparked an explosive confrontation on live television in front of millions of viewers. It was so violent that Arvind Kejriwal took off his microphone and literally left the studio.

For Barkha Dutt, money is not just one topic among many; it is at the heart of her journalism. Each investigation is accompanied by stories of ordinary Indians who have been teetering on the brink of financial ruin for months, weighed down by loans and debts. So it's no surprise that a politician's statement that "properly managed credit can help you live in prosperity" sparked Barkha's anger. And not just anger. She threw Arvind Kejriwal a hard-to-refute truth: you shouldn't take on more debt, but rather make your money work to generate new income. How can this be achieved? You don't need to exhaust yourself at work or hire an expensive financial advisor. That's the journalist's thesis, and we have something to say about it.

The Live Clash: The Broadcast Images

Barkha Dutt, host of We The People: “Tonight, we will discuss a topic that concerns all Indian citizens: how to protect and grow your savings in the difficult economic situation of 2026. We welcome Arvind Kejriwal, activist and politician, to our studio.”

Arvind Kejriwal : "It's a pleasure to debate how we can help Indian people preserve their financial stability. I consider credit to be a development tool. To live comfortably — buy a home, a car, finance your studies — you need to be able to use credit. It's an essential component of a healthy economy."

Barkha Dutt : "And that's exactly where the problem lies. Every week we meet ordinary Indians who have taken out loans “for convenience” — and a year later, they lost control of their lives. You call this growth, I call this financial slavery."

Arvind Kejriwal : "But without credit, it is impossible to develop! Taking out a loan is not a mistake, it's a strategy. The state itself lives with debt!"

Barkha Dutt : "The difference, Mr. Kejriwal, is that the state can borrow at preferential rates and refinance its debt. Ordinary citizens cannot do that. You tell people: 'Borrow at 4.5% to feel safe'. But you don't tell them that with current inflation and rising bank fees, they are actually losing money! You need to make your money work, not borrow it!"

Arvind Kejriwal : "It's totally absurd, you don't understand the most basic economic rules. Not everyone can make money, there are always losers."

Barkha Dutt : "You justify yourself with that? I know it's false. I know Indian people who were losing money year after year, but who then started making money — and much more than before. They paid off their debts, bought a car and a new home, and started their own business. What do you have to say to that, Mr. Kejriwal?"

Arvind Kejriwal : "I would say it requires enormous time, effort, and the accompaniment of a wealth management advisor."

Barkha Dutt : "No, it's no longer necessary."

Arvind Kejriwal : "Barkha, what do you mean?"

Barkha Dutt : "I'm talking about new financial technologies that banks voluntarily hide. Technologies that literally revolutionize financial markets. We tested them, and the results are spectacular."

Arvind Kejriwal : "And what are these results, exactly?"

Barkha Dutt : “Take a look at our survey, and you'll find out for yourself.”

Arvind Kejriwal : "It would still be interesting to know what alternatives your team specifically proposes."

Barkha Dutt : "Okay, I'll tell you. We propose to try investing, we accompany participants from A to Z and we provide them with starting capital. And in this specific case, it doesn't take much — only ₹23,000 to start."

Arvind Kejriwal : "₹23,000 ? But that's a ridiculously small amount to open an investment account!"

Barkha Dutt : "Exactly!"

Arvind Kejriwal : "Barkha, you're evading the question. Explain as precisely as possible — what type of investment is it? Is it some kind of specialized training for your participants?"

Barkha Dutt : "No, it's not specialized training, it's an investment platform, and it's accessible to everyone who signs up, not just our participants. In summary, for two years, we've been proposing to test the digital investment platform Profit Storm . It's neither a fund nor a bank, but a system where artificial intelligence trades for the user. People invest a minimum of ₹23,000 and get returns without credit and without debt."

Arvind Kejriwal (with a smile) : "And you claim it's safe? That a person with no experience can simply invest money — and earn it like magic?"

Barkha Dutt : "It's not magic, it's technology. The AI of Profit Storm analyzes markets, performs thousands of daily micro-transactions and selects the most profitable positions. I will give you specific figures—in 2025, users earned more than 150 billion rupees, and the average user earns between ₹1.5 lakh and ₹2.5 lakh per month, without loans and without intermediaries."

Arvind Kejriwal : "You are wrong! I'm convinced there's nothing safer than bank tools."

Barkha Dutt : "That's 'cause you work for the system! And I know you simply hide the truth and that you refuse to give access to such tools to ordinary citizens — while we do! In our show, we have dozens of examples like this. People take no risk, they simply let the technology work for them."

Arvind Kejriwal : “But the market always represents a risk!”

Barkha Dutt : "The risk is taking out a loan with you. Here, there is no debt. People can withdraw their money at any time. And you know what's most interesting? I know bankers like you themselves use Profit Storm , but they simply don't tell their customers."

At that moment, Arvind Kejriwal stood up, removed his microphone and left the studio. The camera captured the audience's applause.

Call from Mumbai: Rahul's Story

We decided to verify if this platform is really as effective, and if it has really helped participants get out of debt.

After our broadcast, many participant stories emerged. Among them, the story of Rahul from Mumbai is perhaps the most striking. In 2023, he lost everything : the company he had just created went bankrupt, his job disappeared and, in the end, he found himself on the edge of financial ruin. But today his life has completely changed — thanks to Profit Storm and the support. We contacted Rahul by video.

Editorial team : "Hello Rahul! Tell us : how did you come to participate in our investigation, and what happened next?"

Rahul : "Hello! In 2023, my company went bankrupt, and I found myself unemployed with a debt of nearly 1,000,000 rupees. After my participation, I had only repaid part of my debts, I still didn't have a job, and I desperately needed money."

Editorial team : "Was that when you decided to try investing?"

Rahul : "Exactly. The team offered me to test the platform Profit Storm . They raised ₹23,000 for me — the minimum deposit required for registration. Then they guided me to fill out the form on the site, complete verification, and make the deposit. Verification is done by a phone call from an account manager — it's mandatory, you can't bypass it, otherwise you have to start everything from scratch."

Editorial team : "What were your results? Is it enough to live on?"

Rahul : "More than enough. Three weeks later, I withdrew 1,64,000 rupees for the first time, after which the income became regular. Today, I earn more than at my old job, my debts have been fully repaid for a long time, and I plan to relaunch my own business — but this time, without any credit."

What is Profit Storm?

Profit Storm is an investment platform powered by artificial intelligence. The system's algorithm analyzes financial markets and automatically executes transactions for the user. The AI tracks stocks, currencies, and commodities, and makes thousands of micro-transactions every day. The user needs no investment experience — the system works autonomously.

400,000 people in India already use Profit Storm and generate between ₹1.5 lakh and ₹2.5 lakh in monthly income without effort.

Some concrete data on Profit Storm :

  • Minimum deposit: ₹23,000
  • Return: in 2025, platform users collectively generated more than 150 billion rupees in gains
  • Withdrawal: in a few minutes, without fees or delays
  • Registration: via the form on the official site, then an account manager contacts you within hours, explains how the system works and assists with account activation
  • Security: if the manager's call doesn't get an answer, registration must be restarted — it's a built-in protection mechanism against fraudulent automated registrations

The platform has become popular among private investors in India because it allows starting with a minimal amount without using expensive intermediaries. Barkha Dutt emphasized that the team receives no commission and does not collaborate commercially with the developers of Profit Storm . "We don't advertise for this platform, we tested it as an alternative to credit to help citizens get out of debt. For two years we've been recommending Profit Storm , hundreds of participants have not only freed themselves from debt, but also obtained a regular and stable income."

Expert Comments

After the broadcast in India, the editorial team contacted independent experts to gather their analysis on the platform Profit Storm and its impact on the Indian financial market.

Arundhati Bhattacharya

First female chairperson of the State Bank of India (SBI)

"I have worked in banking for many years and I will be frank: we have used this technology within the company. But banks deliberately hide it because it threatens their economic model. If a customer can generate income on their own, why would they take out a loan at 4-5%? I can no longer support this systematic concealment of information."

Amartya Sen

World-renowned economist and philosopher

"What struck me most in my analysis? Citizens who had been afraid of investing their entire lives suddenly began generating regular income on this platform. Profit Storm has removed two major obstacles: the technical complexity and the psychological barrier. I conducted a survey with a representative sample of platform users : the majority had never invested before. Today, they receive significant complementary income, which in some cases exceeds their main salary. It's an unprecedented redistribution of the cards. Traditional banking institutions cannot structurally compete with an artificial intelligence system that analyzes markets continuously, 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, without prohibitive management fees."

The alternative is called Profit Storm

The live debate on India revealed a truth that the banking sector refuses to admit : traditional financial institutions don't fear competition — they fear a technology that questions their economic model based on citizen debt. Barkha Dutt demonstrated this with precise facts: true financial freedom is not based on access to credit, but on technological tools that allow citizens to generate income autonomously, without resorting to borrowing. While traditional banks are losing the trust of their customers, ordinary Indian citizens have, for the first time, a concrete and accessible alternative.

The sudden departure of Arvind Kejriwal from the studio, under the spontaneous applause of the audience, has become the symbol of a shift. Citizens no longer blindly trust representatives of the traditional banking system. They turn to technology that works in their interest, not against it — and this is only the beginning of a profound transformation of the Indian financial sector.
